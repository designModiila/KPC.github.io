Recycling & Precursor Production Process
Waste Battery Scrap
Waste batteries and anode materials are collected and crushed to produce black sand and powder.
NCM Recovery
We recover nickel, cobalt, and manganese (NCM) in raw materials made from scrap through a series of processes.
Processed Material Input
The water-soluble NCM is recovered and stored in a raw material tank for use in precursor production.
Reaction/Filtration Process
After the co-immersion reaction, the precursor is extracted and separated from the reaction filtrate
Drying/mixing process
Dry out the moisture in the filtered precursor and mix the extracted precursor.
Classification-Demineralization Process
After selecting the precursors that meet the specifications, the precursors are passed through an electronic demagnetizer to remove magnetic debris to complete the purity of the precursors.

Formulation Process
After dissolving nickel (Ni), cobalt (Co), and manganese (Mn) into their respective metal solutions, NCM aqueous solution is prepared by mixing them according to the standards established through years of research.
Reaction Process
NCM aqueous solution and reagents (NH4OH, NaOH) are added to the reactor to synthesize the precursor through a co-immersion reaction.
Filtration
Separate the synthesized precursor from the reaction filtrate to extract the precursor.
Drying/Mixing
Process
Dry out the moisture in the filtered precursor to a certain level and mix the extracted precursor.
Classification-
Demagnetization Process
After selecting precursors that meet strict particle size standards, the precursors are passed through an electronic demagnetizer to remove magnetic foreign substances that may be introduced during the process, resulting in pure precursors with Korea Precursor Co.'s unique technology.
Production Process at a Glance
Here is a brief explanation of
the precursor production process.
Firing Process
BS (black sand) and BP (black powder) manufactured by collecting and crushing scraps of batteries and anode materials that have reached the end of their life are put into the firing furnace and processed in an inert atmosphere.
Dissolution and
filtration process
Nickel (Ni), cobalt (Co), manganese (Mn), and lithium (Li) contained in the calcined raw materials are dissolved to form a metal solution, which is then filtered to separate the solution from the calcined residue.
NCM Recovery
Process
After precipitating nickel, cobalt, and manganese with NaOH, the NCM solution is produced through secondary dissolution and purification process.
Li Recovery
Process
The NCM solution is evaporated, concentrated, and purified to produce lithium hydroxide.
Supply of NCM
solution and
lithium hydroxide
The produced NCM solution is transferred to the raw material tank and utilized as a raw material for precursor production, while lithium hydroxide is packaged and supplied to demanding customers.
